### Common Patterns & Diagnostics
1.  **"Buy the Rumor, Sell the News" Drift:** The signal triggered on June 1, 2026, ahead of the scheduled earnings release on June 7. The stock experienced a steady pre-event downward drift. This represents typical market behavior where participants take profits or hedge ahead of highly anticipated, risky announcements.
2.  **Market Direction (Beta):** The absolute return did not account for the broader market index performance (Nifty 50) over the identical time frame. If the index fell by more than 7.38%, the signal actually generated positive relative outperformance (Alpha).
3.  **Lack of Sentiment Directionality:** While news mentions spiked (velocity = 4.00x), the signal engine triggered without evaluating the directional sentiment of the text. A spike in mentions caused by negative rumors or warnings will still trigger a buy signal under the current rules, creating a false positive.

## 6. Recommendations

### Should MARKET_INTEL continue generating these signals?
**Yes, but with critical enhancements.** The current sample size (3 signals, 1 completed) is too small to draw terminal conclusions. However, executing unhedged absolute signals without sentiment or benchmark filters is highly risky.

### Which signal types should be strengthened?
1.  **Results & Board Meetings:** These are high-impact corporate catalysts. We must strengthen them by:
    *   **Sentiment Filtering:** Restrict signals to trigger only when the Ollama LLM registers a `Positive` sentiment and `Impact Score >= 3`.
    *   **Benchmark Relative Normalization:** Implement Nifty 50 relative returns immediately to isolate pure Alpha ($\alpha$).

### Which signal types should be removed?
*   **None currently.** We should maintain the current categories to collect more backtest data, but we must strictly avoid adding raw news velocity spikes as standalone buy entries without corporate event convergence.
